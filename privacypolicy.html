Last Updated: March 2026

360 (“we”, “our”, “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy and providing a safe experience for all users. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our website, services, and features.

1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide

  • Account information (email, username, password)
  • Profile details (avatar, display name, tags)

  • Content you submit through forms or tools

Information Collected Automatically

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and settings
  • Usage data (pages visited, actions taken)
  • Cookies and similar technologies

Third‑Party Authentication

If you sign in with Google or GitHub, we receive:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Profile picture

We do not receive your password from these providers.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide and improve our services
  • Authenticate users

  • Analyze usage to improve performance

We never sell your personal information.

3. How We Share Information

We may share information with:

  • Service providers (e.g., our backends and APIs) to operate core features

  • Analytics tools to understand usage patterns

We do not share your data with advertisers.

4. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored securely using trusted third‑party infrastructure (Supabase). We use encryption, access controls, and industry‑standard security practices.

No system is 100% secure, but we work to protect your information.

5. Your Rights

You may have the right to:

  • Access your data
  • Update or correct your data
  • Delete your account
  • Request a copy of your data

To exercise these rights, contact us at: help@360-search.com

6. Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Keep you logged in
  • Remember your preferences
  • Improve site performance

You can disable cookies in your browser settings.

360 is not intended for children under the age of 7. We do not knowingly collect information from children, and we do not encourage parents to have their child(ren) use it. We are not responsible for any information they find that the family deems “inappropriate”.

8. Changes to This Policy
